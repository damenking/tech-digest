OpenRouter raises $113M Series B
OpenRouter, the API aggregation layer that lets developers route requests across dozens of AI model providers from a single interface, has raised a $113 million Series B. This is a significant milestone for what started as a relatively niche developer tool. OpenRouter's value proposition is straightforward: instead of integrating with OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, and others separately, you hit one API and can switch models with a parameter change. For developers building AI-powered products, this dramatically reduces vendor lock-in risk and makes it easy to compare models on cost and performance. The raise signals that infrastructure plays in the AI stack are attracting serious capital, not just the model labs themselves. OpenRouter sits at an interesting strategic position — it's deeply useful to developers but also somewhat at the mercy of the model providers, who could theoretically offer better pricing or terms for direct integration. The $113M will likely go toward expanding their model catalog, improving reliability and latency, and possibly adding more sophisticated routing logic like automatic fallback and cost optimization. For anyone building on multiple AI providers, this is a tool worth having in your stack.

AV2 video standard hits final v1.0 specification
The AV2 video codec standard has reached its final v1.0 specification release, a significant milestone for the open, royalty-free video codec space. AV2 is the successor to AV1, developed by the Alliance for Open Media — the consortium that includes Google, Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Netflix, and others. AV1 itself was a major achievement, delivering substantially better compression than H.264 at no licensing cost, and AV2 promises further efficiency gains. Better compression matters in multiple domains: lower streaming bandwidth costs for video platforms, better video quality at constrained bitrates for mobile users, and reduced storage requirements. The transition from AV1 to AV2 will be gradual — hardware decoder support needs to be built into chips before AV2 can be used efficiently on consumer devices without draining battery. The final specification release is the starting gun for hardware and software vendors to begin implementing. For developers working on video infrastructure, now is the time to start understanding AV2's capabilities and planning for eventual adoption. The codec wars between open formats like AV2 and proprietary formats like H.266/VVC will continue, but the industry weight behind the Alliance for Open Media gives AV2 strong momentum.

For engineers interested in robotics, automotive manufacturing is one of the best real-world test beds for humanoid robots because the tasks are well-defined, the facilities are large, and the companies have both the capital and the incentive to invest. Law enforcement has dismantled a botnet of more than 17 million compromised devices that was reportedly tied to a Russia-based residential proxy network. Residential proxy networks are particularly insidious because they route malicious traffic through real consumer devices — home routers, IoT devices, computers — making the traffic look like it's coming from legitimate residential ISP addresses rather than data centers. This makes detection and blocking much harder for targets. A network of 17 million devices is enormous and represents significant infrastructure for cybercriminal operations ranging from credential stuffing and ad fraud to more sophisticated attacks. The Russia nexus, while not definitively meaning state involvement, puts this in the context of ongoing geopolitical cyber tensions. For security practitioners, this is a reminder of the scale at which botnet infrastructure operates and why device security hygiene — keeping firmware updated, changing default credentials, segmenting IoT devices on separate networks — matters. The takedown is a genuine win for law enforcement and the organizations that were being targeted, though historically botnet operators have shown the ability to rebuild after disruptions. The specifics of how the takedown was executed and which agencies were involved are worth following as more details emerge.
