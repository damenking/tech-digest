The move is frustrating because Cloudflare has historically presented itself as an infrastructure player that takes user privacy seriously, and Turnstile was a genuine step forward from Google's reCAPTCHA. The practical effect is that any site using Turnstile now imposes a meaningful privacy cost on users who try to avoid fingerprinting. For developers choosing between CAPTCHA solutions, this changes the calculus — Turnstile is no longer clearly the privacy-friendly choice. The broader issue is that bot detection and user privacy are in genuine tension, and there may not be a solution that perfectly satisfies both; but requiring hardware fingerprinting without disclosure or opt-out is a meaningful step backward.
