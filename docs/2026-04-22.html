Anthropic's Mythos AI Found 271 Zero-Day Vulnerabilities in Firefox 150 — And Someone May Have Stolen It

Preserved for Billions of Years: Diverse Organic Molecules Discovered on Mars
NASA's Mars Science Laboratory has returned results from the first SAM TMAH (Tetramethylammonium Hydroxide) thermochemolysis experiment conducted by the Curiosity rover, revealing a diverse array of organic molecules preserved in Martian rock for billions of years. This is a genuinely significant scientific result: the detection of diverse organics — not just simple molecules but a varied suite — strengthens the case that Mars had conditions capable of preserving complex chemistry over geological timescales. TMAH derivatization is a technique that helps break down and analyze organic compounds that might otherwise be difficult to detect, so this represents a methodological advance in Mars chemistry as well as a scientific discovery. The big open question, as always, is whether these organics are evidence of past life, abiotic chemistry, or contamination from meteorites — and the current data doesn't settle that, but it sharpens the hypothesis space. For the astrobiology community, this keeps Mars firmly in play as a target for biosignature research.
