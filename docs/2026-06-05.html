Google's deal with Voltus for virtual power plants for demand response (covered separately by MIT Tech Review) fits into this same broader picture of hyperscalers trying to be more thoughtful about resource footprints, at least partly in response to regulatory and community pressure. The transition to direct liquid cooling for AI chips — driven by the thermal density of GPU clusters — is actually helping on the water front for some operators, since liquid cooling can be done with closed loops. The industry is moving, but driven more by cost and regulation than pure environmental motivation, which is worth being clear-eyed about.

Dashlane Discloses How Attackers Downloaded Encrypted Password Vaults
Dashlane published a post-mortem explaining how attackers managed to exfiltrate encrypted password vaults from a significant number of users. The attack strategy relied on targeting a large volume of accounts simultaneously to increase the probability of success — a statistical approach rather than a targeted one. The vaults are encrypted, which means the immediate risk depends heavily on the strength of users' master passwords; short or common master passwords could be brute-forced offline now that attackers have the vault files. This follows a pattern established by the LastPass breach in late 2022, which taught the industry that encrypted vault theft is a serious threat model even when the encryption itself isn't broken at the time of the breach. For security professionals, this is a useful case study in the threat model around password manager architecture and the limits of encryption-at-rest as a sole protection. Dashlane's transparency in publishing the post-mortem is commendable and worth noting — many companies in similar situations have been far less forthcoming. Users affected should treat this as a strong signal to rotate any passwords for high-value accounts, enable hardware security keys where possible, and consider whether their master password is strong enough to resist offline attack. The broader industry lesson is that password manager vault storage needs defense-in-depth beyond just encryption.
